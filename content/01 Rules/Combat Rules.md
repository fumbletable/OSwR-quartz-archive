# Combat Rules

## 1. Combat Overview

Combat in Olde Swords Reign is fast-paced and tactical, following a structured sequence:

1. Surprise: surprised creatures can’t act on round 1.
2. Initiative: d20 + DEX.
3. On your turn: move up to your speed and take one Action.
4. Reactions: max 1/round (see below).
5. End of round: resolve ongoing effects.

This sequence ensures smooth and dynamic encounters.

---

## 2. Attacks and Damage

- Attack Rolls: Roll d20 + PB (if applicable) + relevant modifier (Strength for melee, Dexterity for ranged). Hit if total ≥ target’s AC.
- Damage: Roll weapon/spell damage + modifiers. Subtract from the target's hit points.
- Critical Hits: Natural 20 doubles damage dice.
- Critical Failures: Natural 1 may cause unintended consequences -Referee's discretion (opportunity attack against you edge on next attack against you, set back on your next attack...).  

---

## 3. Combat Actions

On your turn, you can perform one action:

- Attack: Melee or ranged.
- Cast a Spell: Requires preparation and conditions (e.g., line of sight).
- Dodge: Enemies attacking you have a SETBACK until your next turn.
- Disengage: Break Melee and DASH without provoking opportunity attacks.
- Dash: Double your movement for the turn.
- Ready: Prepare an action triggered by a condition.
- Grapple/Shove: STR vs STR/DEX (winner imposes restrained/prone or pushes 5 ft; apply ED/SD from position).

### Reactions

- Opportunity Attack: when a foe leaves your melee reach (usually CLOSE/5 ft; Reach weapons use 10 ft) without Disengaging.
- Feats may grant other reactions.

### Unseen attackers (replace A/D)
- Attacking from hidden: attacker gains EDGE.
- Attacking while blinded/smoke: attacker suffers SETBACK.
- Apply the standard 1 ED / 1 SD situational cap; cancel if both apply.

---

## 4. Movement and Positioning


- Slow: 15 ft (e.g., encumbered).
- Normal: 30 ft. 
- Fast: 45 ft (e.g., sprinting).

- Difficult Terrain: Costs double movement speed.


### Common position calls

- Higher ground - EDGE
- 2 or more melee attacking a single target - EDGE
- Smoke, darkness, slippery ledge: impose SETBACK.
- Hidden attacker gains EDGE; blinded attacker suffers SETBACK (they cancel if both apply).

## 6. Death and Dying

## 6. Death and Dying

Two options are provided here. Use whichever best fits your table.  
Your Referee may also substitute another process (see Appendix).

---

### Option A — Single d20 Table
When you hit **0 HP**, roll **1d20** (no Boost, no EDGE):

|   d20 | Result                                                                                                                                     |
| ----: | ------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------ |
|   1–2 | **Death** — you’re gone.                                                                                                                   |
|   3–4 | **Bleeding Out** — you die at the end of your next turn unless stabilised. If stabilised: wake at 1 HP in `d8` rounds and gain +2 Fatigue. |
|   5–7 | **Major Collapse** — wake at 1 HP in `d6` rounds and gain +2 Fatigue.                                                                      |
|   8–9 | **Minor Collapse** — wake at 1 HP in `d4` rounds and gain +1 Fatigue.                                                                      |
| 10–20 | **Rally** — wake at 1 HP at the start of your next turn.                                                                                   |

**Stabilising Bleeding Out:** Adjacent ally uses **Action + 1 Provision** and makes a **WIS or INT check (DC 12)**. Success = stabilised (apply result above). 

---

### Option B — Death Saves with Fatigue
When you hit **0 HP**, you fall unconscious and start making **death saves** at the start of your turns:

- Roll **d20**: 10+ = success, 9− = failure.  
- On 3 successes → you are stable (wake at 1 HP after 1d4 hours).  
- On 3 failures → you die.  
- **Natural 20** = back up at 1 HP immediately.  
- **Natural 1** = instant death.  
- **Each failed save also gives +1 Fatigue.**

**Damage while down:** If you take damage at 0 HP → 1 failed save (or 2 if the hit is melee within 5 ft). If the damage ≥ your max HP beyond 0, you die outright.

**Stabilising:** Adjacent ally uses **Action + 1 Provision** and makes a **WIS check (DC 12)**. Success = stable (no more death saves, wake at 1 HP after 1d4 hours).  

---

### Notes
- **Fatigue stacking** makes repeated collapses harsher even if you survive.
